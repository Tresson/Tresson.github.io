PERSONA CLUSTERS

ALL BY Cluster
Cluster 1
Personas 11/56 (7,600/80,000)
This cluster represents women in their early to mid-20s, unmarried daughters. Many worked the lands to help feed their mothers, fathers, brothers, and sisters, or tended the needs around the house.
Cluster 2
Personas 12/56 (12,700/80,000)
This cluster is the second most populated group in the dataset, and represents the few who were able to obtain independence in the south. In order to take care of themselves and family members, most of these women took on jobs doing domestic tasks.
Cluster 3
Personas 10/56 (5,600/80,000)
This group contains individuals whom live as a lodger (renting a room for long-term stay). Typically, unmarried and works in the domestic field, the women in this cluster are independent, many of which have children.
Cluster 4
Personas 8/56 (2,200/80,000)
The smallest cluster, primarily focuses on women whom live with other relatives, or people outside of the nuclear family. Agricultural and domestic jobs represent a split focus of occupations for this group.
Cluster 5
Personas 15/56 (51,900/80,000)
Representing by far the largest persona group, the women here are married, with children and work in the home. Due to the educational and societal limits placed upon former slaves at this time, very few opportunities outside of the defining cluster were possible.
